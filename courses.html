Robert Kooima

I am an Adjunct Professor of Computer Science at Louisiana State University and a post-doctorial researcher at the Center for Computation & Technology. I study real-time 3D scientific visualization, focusing on virtual reality and GPU programming.

This site catalogs my work. It includes courses taught, research done, talks given, installations, demonstrations, and handy source code.

My curriculum vitae includes all of my published and co-authored papers.

CSC 4356 / ME 4573 Interactive Computer Graphics

Fall 2010

Interactive Computer Graphics provides an in-depth introduction to real-time 3D graphics programming using OPENGL and the C or C++ programming language. Here's a poster advertising the course and showing a few of the concepts demonstrated. Much of the source code used in the class may be found on my code page, including most of the demonstrations shown during lectures.

CSC 4263 / ART 4020 Video Game Design

Spring 2009, 2010, 2011

Video Game Design is a team-based, project-centric course that covers every aspect of video game design and development. This course is taught collaboratively as CS 426 with Prof. Jason Leigh at the University of Illinois at Chicago via high-definition video teleconference. Here's a poster advertising the course and displaying some of the games developed for the TacTile Table in 2009 and 2010.
