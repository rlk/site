Robert Kooima

I am an Adjunct Professor of Computer Science at Louisiana State University and a post-doctorial researcher at the Center for Computation & Technology. I study real-time 3D scientific visualization, focusing on virtual reality and GPU programming.

This site catalogs my work. It includes courses taught, research done, talks given, installations, demonstrations, and handy source code.

My curriculum vitae includes all of my published and co-authored papers.

Tellurion on the StarCAVE at Calit2, UCSD

October 2007—present

Tellurion is on display using the StarCAVE at Calit2 on the campus of the University of California, San Diego. A direct descendant of EVL's CAVE design, the StarCAVE is a 17-screen cylindrical virtual reality display. It uses 34 JVC projectors at 1920×1080 to produce circularly polarized passive stereo 3D on 5 walls and the floor. Each pair of projectors is driven by a PC with an NVIDIA Quadro 5600. 6 degree-of-freedom head and hand tracking is provided by an ART motion tracking system. In this image, the fifth wall slides forward on rails to enclose the environment.

Tellurion on the Varrier at Calit2, UCSD

August 2007—present

Tellurion is on display using the 60-tile Varrier at Calit2 on the campus of the University of California, San Diego. This installation has a 12×5 array of 21-inch LCD panels, each with a resolution of 1600×1200. Linescreen resolution is 536 lines per foot, which affords an effective visual acuity better than 20/30. The display is driven by a cluster 16 PCs, each with a pair of NVIDIA GeForce 7900GTXs.

Electro: Mars at the Adler Planetarium

January 2007—present

Mars is on exhibit at the Adler Planetarium in Chicago in the Our Solar System Gallery. The installation uses three LCD displays at 1600×1200 driven by a single NVIDIA GeForce 8800 via a Matrox TripleHead2Go. The interaction is demonstrated by an Adler guest in this YouTube video.

Electro: Mars at Calit2, UCSD

2006—2010

For a number of years Mars was the primary demonstration of the 60-tile Varrier at Calit2 on the campus of the University of California, San Diego. It was the first application to be shown on this Varrier, and the construction of this display provided the motivation for the creation of the Mars visualization during the summer of 2006.

Autostereo Telecon at iGrid2005 and SC'05

September and November 2005

The Autostereo Video Telecommunication application was demonstrated at iGrid2005 in San Diego and SC'05 in Seattle. In both cases, stereo video was streamed to and from an identical installation at EVL in Chicago.
